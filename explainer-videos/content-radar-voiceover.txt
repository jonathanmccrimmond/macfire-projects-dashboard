This is the Content Radar review calendar as it is today.

The calendar shows draft posts that are waiting for review. Each item is tied to a date, so you can see what is coming up and what still needs approval.

Click a draft on the calendar to review it.

On the right, check the title, the date, the source, and the freshness label before approving anything.

Then check the platform tabs. Facebook is written a little more casually. LinkedIn is more professional. X is shorter.

If you are posting manually, click Copy text for the platform you want.

If the wording is right, click Approve. That means the post is safe to move forward.

If it does not sound right, click Decline. That stops it from going into the posting flow.

For now, approved posts can be copied and posted manually, or used as the trigger for Jonathan or Doogie to post on the scheduled day.

Later, the Social Media Agent can take approved posts from this calendar and publish them automatically on the selected date.

The important point is control. Nothing should go out just because the system wrote it. The review step is where MacFire decides what is safe to publish.
