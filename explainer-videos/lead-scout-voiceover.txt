This is the AI Lead Scout dashboard as it is today.

Start with the summary cards at the top. Total Leads is the full database. High Priority shows the stronger matches. Qualified and Ready is the queue to check first.

Click Qualified. This is the working list for leads that look worth reviewing.

On each lead card, start with the company name and score. Then check the sector, postcode, source, contact information, and the short reasoning line.

If you want to sense check the premises, use Maps or Street View before doing anything else.

When you have reached out to the business, click Mark Contacted. That keeps the lead in the pipeline, but shows it is no longer untouched.

If the conversation becomes a meeting, click Meeting Booked. That lets the dashboard track which leads are turning into real opportunities.

If the lead is clearly not a fit, click Not Relevant. That drops it out of the working queue, so you do not keep reviewing the same bad lead.

Nothing is permanently lost. Open the Not Relevant tab to see what has been removed.

If something was moved there by mistake, click Add back to queue. The lead returns to the review queue.

Contact Needed is separate. Use that tab for strong leads where the system still needs better phone, email, or website details.

The weekly routine is simple. Check Qualified first, use Maps or Street View if needed, mark contacted leads, drop poor fits into Not Relevant, and keep the queue clean.
