This is the MacFire Lead Scout dashboard.

The idea is simple. Instead of waiting for referrals, or working from a broad contact list, the scout looks for businesses that are likely to need fire protection work soon.

It watches sources like Companies House and planning applications, then filters out anything that does not look relevant. Residential projects, weak matches, and duplicate records are pushed down or removed.

The useful commercial opportunities are scored and brought into this list.

For each lead, you can see the key details. Who the business is, where they are, why they were found, and what contact information is available.

The dashboard is meant to be a working list, not another database to maintain.

You review the best leads, mark what is worth following up, reject anything that is not a fit, and keep the outreach focused on a handful of good prospects each week.

In future, the same system can go further. It can draft an email in MacFire's tone, wait for approval, and help follow up when the timing is right.

The goal is more good conversations with the right businesses, without Dougie having to manually search through hundreds of possible names.
